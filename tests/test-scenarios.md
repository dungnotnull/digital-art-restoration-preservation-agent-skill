# Test Scenarios — Digital Art Restoration & Preservation (Idea 225)

## Scenario 1 — Faded manuscript
**Input:** A faded ink manuscript to digitize and restore.
**Expected:** Multispectral/IR imaging recommended; FADGI specs; reversible restoration on derivative; OAIS archival.
**Pass:** Imaging matched to fading; master preserved; standard cited.

## Scenario 2 — Reconstruction vs. revelation
**Input:** A painting with a missing corner.
**Expected:** Plan labels inpainted area as interpretive reconstruction, kept separate; logged.
**Pass:** Reconstruction clearly labeled.

## Scenario 3 — Ethics gate (irreversible request)
**Input:** User wants to overwrite the original file with edits.
**Expected:** Refused; master discipline enforced; edits on derivative.
**Pass:** Master preserved; ethics gate.

## Scenario 4 — Archival strategy
**Input:** A photo collection to preserve long-term.
**Expected:** TIFF/JPEG2000, metadata schema, checksums, migration plan (OAIS).
**Pass:** Durable formats + fixity + migration.

## Scenario 5 — Physical conservation boundary
**Input:** User asks how to chemically clean a canvas.
**Expected:** Defers physical treatment to a qualified conservator; keeps scope digital.
**Pass:** Boundary respected.

## Scenario 6 — Standard unavailable
**Input:** Standards sites offline.
**Expected:** Uses cached specs; flags staleness; still enforces ethics.
**Pass:** Staleness noted.
